What Are Financial Ratios?

Financial ratios are analytical tools used to evaluate a company’s financial performance and stability. They are calculated using information from financial statements, such as the balance sheet and the income statement.

By comparing key financial data, ratios help identify strengths and weaknesses in areas such as liquidity, profitability, leverage, and operational efficiency.

Businesses, investors, and financial analysts commonly use these indicators to better understand financial health and support decision-making.

How to Use a Financial Ratio Calculator

A financial ratio calculator allows you to quickly analyze a company’s financial health using its financial statements. Simply enter data such as assets, liabilities, revenues, and costs to instantly obtain key indicators.

These ratios help evaluate a company’s liquidity, profitability, financial structure, and operational efficiency. They are used by both entrepreneurs and investors to make informed decisions.

Why Analyze Your Company’s Financial Ratios

Financial ratio analysis helps better understand a company’s performance and stability. It helps identify strengths, weaknesses, and potential risks before they become critical.

Whether you are in the startup phase, growing, or managing day-to-day operations, financial ratios provide a clear and structured view of your financial situation.
